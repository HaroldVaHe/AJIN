# What to Do After Wrongful Termination in Colombia

Being fired from your job is a difficult situation that creates uncertainty. However, Colombian law protects your labor rights. Here is a step-by-step guide.

## What is wrongful termination?

It occurs when the employer ends the employment contract without a valid legal reason as established in Article 62 of the Substantive Labor Code.

## Your Rights

When wrongfully terminated, you are entitled to:

- **Compensation**: Between 15 and 180 days of salary depending on seniority and contract type
- **Settlement**: Full social benefits
- **Service bonus**
- **Severance funds and interest**
- **Unused vacation days**

## Steps to Take

### 1. Do not sign anything immediately

Before signing any document, consult with an attorney. Many people sign resignations or agreements that limit their rights without knowing it.

### 2. Gather evidence

- Termination letter (if any)
- Employment contract
- Recent pay stubs
- Emails or communications

### 3. Calculate your compensation

The law establishes a progressive table:

- **Less than 1 year**: 15 days of salary
- **Between 1 and 5 years**: 20 days + 15 days per additional year
- **More than 5 years**: 40 days + 20 days per additional year

### 4. File a lawsuit

You have **3 years** to claim your labor rights before the ordinary courts.
